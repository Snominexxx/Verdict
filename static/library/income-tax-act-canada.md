## Overview

The *Income Tax Act* (R.S.C., 1985, c. 1 (5th Supp.)) is Canada's primary statute imposing income tax on individuals, corporations, and trusts. It is one of the longest and most complex Canadian statutes.

## Structure

- **Part I** — Income Tax (the core: income computation, deductions, credits)
- **Part I.1–I.3** — Surtaxes (largely historical)
- **Part II** — Tobacco Manufacturers' Surtax
- **Part III** — Additional Tax on Excessive Elections
- **Part IV** — Tax on Dividends Received by Private Corporations
- **Part V–XV** — Various special taxes and rules
- **Part XVI** — Administration and Enforcement
- **Part XVII** — Interpretation

## Key Concepts

- **Residence** — Individuals and corporations resident in Canada are taxed on worldwide income.
- **Sources of Income** — Employment, business, property, capital gains, other.
- **Deductions** — RRSP contributions, childcare, moving expenses, etc.
- **Tax Credits** — Basic personal amount, charitable donations, medical expenses, etc.
- **Anti-Avoidance** — General Anti-Avoidance Rule (GAAR) at s. 245.

## Sample Provisions

**Section 3** — Income for a taxation year: aggregate of income from each source.

**Section 9** — Business income: profit from a business for the year.

**Section 245** — GAAR: a transaction that results in a tax benefit may be denied if it is an avoidance transaction that misuses or abuses the Act.
