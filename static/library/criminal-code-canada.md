## Overview

The *Criminal Code* (R.S.C., 1985, c. C-46) is Canada's primary federal statute codifying criminal offences, defences, procedures, and sentencing.

## Structure

- **Part I** — General (jurisdiction, parties to offences, defences)
- **Part II** — Offences Against Public Order
- **Part II.1** — Terrorism
- **Part III** — Firearms and Other Weapons
- **Part IV** — Offences Against the Administration of Law and Justice
- **Part V** — Sexual Offences, Public Morals
- **Part VI** — Invasion of Privacy
- **Part VII** — Disorderly Houses, Gaming, and Betting
- **Part VIII** — Offences Against the Person (assault, homicide)
- **Part IX** — Offences Against Rights of Property (theft, fraud)
- **Part X** — Fraudulent Transactions
- **Part XI** — Wilful and Forbidden Acts (mischief, arson)
- **Part XII** — Currency Offences
- **Part XII.2** — Proceeds of Crime
- **Part XIII–XXVIII** — Procedure, Sentencing, Appeals

## Key Provisions

**Section 265** — Assault: applying force intentionally without consent.

**Section 322** — Theft: fraudulently taking anything with intent to deprive.

**Section 380** — Fraud: deceit, falsehood, or other fraudulent means to deprive the public or any person of property, money, or services.

**Section 718** — Sentencing purposes: denunciation, deterrence, separation, rehabilitation, reparations, responsibility.
