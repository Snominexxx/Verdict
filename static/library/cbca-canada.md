## Overview

The *Canada Business Corporations Act* (R.S.C., 1985, c. C-44) governs federally incorporated corporations in Canada.

## Key Topics

- **Incorporation** — Articles of incorporation filed with Corporations Canada
- **Corporate Governance** — Directors' duties (care, loyalty), officers, meetings
- **Shareholders** — Rights, proxies, requisitions, unanimous shareholder agreements
- **Securities** — Issuance, transfer, insider trading
- **Fundamental Changes** — Amendments, amalgamations, arrangements, continuance
- **Remedies** — Oppression remedy, derivative actions, compliance orders
- **Liquidation and Dissolution**

## Notable Sections

**Section 102** — Board manages or supervises management of business and affairs.

**Section 122** — Directors and officers must act honestly and in good faith with a view to the best interests of the corporation; exercise the care, diligence, and skill of a reasonably prudent person.

**Section 241** — Oppression remedy: court may make any order it thinks fit to rectify oppressive or unfairly prejudicial conduct.
