## Overview

The *Code of Civil Procedure* (CQLR c C-25.01) governs the conduct of civil litigation before Quebec courts. It covers:

- **Book I** — General Framework (access to justice, proportionality, case management)
- **Book II** — Non-Contentious Proceedings
- **Book III** — Contentious Proceedings (pleadings, evidence, trial)
- **Book IV** — Private Dispute Prevention and Resolution (mediation, arbitration)
- **Book V** — Appeal Procedures
- **Book VI** — Execution of Judgments
- **Book VII** — International Private Procedure
- **Book VIII** — Final and Transitional Provisions

## Key Principles

1. **Proportionality** (art. 18): Proceedings must be proportionate to the nature and complexity of the matter.
2. **Duty to Cooperate** (art. 20): Parties must cooperate and keep one another informed.
3. **Case Management** (art. 148+): Courts actively manage timelines and protocols.

## Sample Articles

**Article 1** — The Code establishes the general framework of civil procedure and the rules applicable before the courts of Quebec.

**Article 9** — The courts and all persons who participate in proceedings must see that they are conducted in a spirit of cooperation and balance.
