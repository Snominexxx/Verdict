## Overview

The *Act Respecting the Legal Publicity of Enterprises* (CQLR c P-44.1) requires businesses operating in Quebec to register with the *Registraire des entreprises* and keep their information up to date.

## Key Obligations

1. **Registration** — Most businesses must file an initial declaration within 60 days of commencing activities.
2. **Annual Updates** — Registrants must file an annual update to confirm or correct their information.
3. **Public Disclosure** — Registered information (name, address, directors, shareholders with 25%+ shares) is publicly accessible.
4. **Striking Off** — Failure to update may result in striking off the register, which can limit the ability to sue.

## Penalties

- Late filing fees
- Administrative striking off
- Inability to invoke rights in court while unregistered (s. 98)
