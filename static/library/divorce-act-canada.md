## Overview

The *Divorce Act* (R.S.C., 1985, c. 3 (2nd Supp.)) is the federal statute governing divorce, parenting orders, child support, and spousal support in Canada.

## Key Concepts

### Grounds for Divorce (s. 8)

- **Separation** — Spouses have lived separate and apart for at least one year.
- **Adultery** — One spouse committed adultery.
- **Cruelty** — Physical or mental cruelty rendering continued cohabitation intolerable.

### Best Interests of the Child (s. 16)

Parenting orders must consider only the best interests of the child, including:

- The child's physical, emotional, and psychological safety
- The child's relationships with parents, siblings, grandparents
- Each parent's willingness to support the child's relationship with the other parent
- History of family violence

### Child Support (s. 15.1)

Determined primarily by the *Federal Child Support Guidelines* based on the payor's income and number of children.

### Spousal Support (s. 15.2)

Considers: length of cohabitation, functions performed during marriage, economic advantages/disadvantages arising from the marriage, self-sufficiency goal.
